Why aPeer

A coordination tool, not a social network

Group chats, spreadsheets, and text threads create ghosting and "who is in" chaos. aPeer replaces that with one deterministic game state.

Deterministic state

aPeer removes ambiguity from pickup games. State is explicit, visible, and shared by everyone.

Commitments are counted

Replies are noise. Confirmations are counted. Quorum is numeric, not interpretive.

Thread replacement

Stop asking "who is in" across WhatsApp and iMessage. One roster, one status line, one answer.

No ghosting blind spots

Late movement shows up as state and roster changes, so hosts can react before the game collapses.

Built for pickup execution

No feed, no scrolling, no content loop. Post or join, verify state, and play.

Why this exists

Uncertainty is expensive

Court reservations get paid whether the game happens or not. When quorum is unclear, time, money, and effort are lost before the first point.

Coordination is fragmented

Plans split across WhatsApp groups, iMessage threads, and spreadsheet checklists. No one sees one current state.

Last-minute dropout risk

A late withdrawal can strand players at the venue with sunk reservation cost and no playable roster.

Certainty changes outcomes

When players can verify status and confirmed roster in seconds, commitment improves and more games reach GAME ON.
